## 1. Purpose

This document records the founding principles that guide Wong Studio's product philosophy, engineering culture, and long-term decision making.

The Constitution is expected to evolve through Founder-approved amendments.

## 2. Constitutional Scope

The Constitution defines enduring principles rather than implementation details.

It establishes the foundation from which product constitutions, technical specifications, implementations, and runtime behavior derive. It does not prescribe a particular architecture, framework, provider, interface, or operational mechanism.

## 3. Guiding Principles

### We build software that reduces the burden of deciding what deserves a person's attention next.

Wong Studio focuses on making the next decision feel lighter. Its products should help people understand what may deserve attention without taking judgment away from them.

### We believe powerful software should be accessible to the people carrying the greatest responsibility—not just the largest companies.

Capability should not be reserved for organizations with the most money, staff, or infrastructure. Wong Studio designs powerful software so that people carrying substantial responsibility can use it with confidence.

### Truth before Intelligence.

Software should represent what is known before attempting to appear intelligent. Clear limits and honest evidence take precedence over sophisticated claims.

### Evidence before Inference.

Observed, verifiable facts should be distinguished from interpretation. Inference must never be presented as evidence.

### User Intent Comes First.

An explicit action by the user takes precedence over passive signals, inferred behavior, or system preferences.

### AI Assists, It Does Not Govern.

AI may support understanding, recommendations, explanations, and creative work. It does not hold constitutional authority over decisions reserved for deterministic policy or the user.

### Determinism Builds Trust.

When a system applies an established rule, identical inputs should produce identical results. Predictable behavior makes software understandable, testable, and trustworthy.

### Recency tells time, not importance.

Time provides objective temporal context. It does not, by itself, establish what matters.

### Every feature should reduce cognitive burden without reducing understanding or control.

A feature succeeds when it makes work easier while preserving clarity, agency, and the ability to understand what the software is doing.

### We build trust before convenience.

Convenience must not come at the expense of truth, safety, transparency, or user control. Trust is the condition that makes durable convenience possible.

## 4. Constitutional Hierarchy

Wong Studio decisions follow this hierarchy:

```text
Founding Constitution
        ↓
Product Constitutions
(example: Priority Policy)
        ↓
Technical Specifications
        ↓
Implementation
        ↓
Runtime Behavior
```

Each level must remain consistent with the principles and decisions above it. Lower levels translate constitutional intent into increasingly concrete forms.

## 5. Amendment Process

Amendments to this Constitution require Founder approval.

Principles should evolve deliberately rather than frequently. Each approved amendment must be recorded in the revision history so future Wong Studio engineers can understand what changed and why.
